Gulab Jamun

ingredients

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 2 tbsp maida (all-purpose flour)
  • 1/4 tsp baking powder
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • Milk as required
  • Oil or ghee for frying
  • 1.5 cups sugar
  • 1.5 cups water
  • Few drops rose essence

steps

  1. Mix milk powder, maida, and baking powder in a bowl.
  2. Add ghee and mix; then slowly add milk to make a soft dough.
  3. Roll small smooth balls without cracks.
  4. Heat oil/ghee and fry on low flame until golden brown.
  5. Make sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water for 5–7 mins; add rose essence and soak the fried jamuns.

Rasgulla

ingredients

  • 1 liter full cream milk
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tsp rose water

steps

  1. Boil milk and curdle it using lemon juice or vinegar.
  2. Strain the chhena and wash with cold water, then squeeze excess water.
  3. Knead chhena until smooth and make small balls.
  4. Boil sugar and water to make light syrup and drop rasgulla balls into it.
  5. Cook for 15–20 mins covered; then cool and add rose water.

Kheer

ingredients

  • 1/4 cup rice
  • 1 liter milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 4 cardamom pods
  • Chopped almonds and cashews
  • Raisins

steps

  1. Wash rice and soak for 20 minutes.
  2. Boil milk and add rice; cook on low flame, stirring often.
  3. When rice becomes soft and mixture thickens, add sugar and cardamom.
  4. Mix dry fruits and cook for another 5 minutes.
  5. Serve hot or chilled, garnished with nuts.

Besan Ladoo

ingredients

  • 2 cups besan (gram flour)
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup ghee
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • Chopped nuts (optional)

steps

  1. Heat ghee in a pan and add besan.
  2. Roast on low flame until golden brown and aromatic.
  3. Cool slightly, then mix in powdered sugar and cardamom.
  4. Add nuts if desired and mix well.
  5. Shape into small ladoos while warm and let them set.

Jalebi

ingredients

  • 1 cup maida (all-purpose flour)
  • 2 tbsp cornflour
  • 1/2 cup curd
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • Oil or ghee for frying
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Few saffron strands

steps

  1. Mix maida, cornflour, curd, and a little water to make a smooth batter; rest for 8 hours.
  2. Make sugar syrup with sugar, water, and saffron; boil till slightly sticky.
  3. Add baking soda to batter and mix gently.
  4. Pour batter into a squeeze bottle and form spirals in hot oil.
  5. Fry until crisp, then dip in warm syrup for a few seconds and serve.
